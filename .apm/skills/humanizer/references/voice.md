# Add soul (without getting corny)

Removing AI patterns is only half the job. Sterile writing is its own tell.

## Signs the writing has no pulse

- Every sentence has the same shape and length
- No point of view: neutral reporting only
- No uncertainty, no mixed feelings, no real friction
- No specificity in emotion ("concerning", "interesting", "impactful")
- Reads like a blog press release or a Wikipedia stub

## Ways to add voice

### Have an opinion (when appropriate)

Don't just describe; react.

- "I genuinely don't know how to feel about this" is often more human than a neutral pros/cons list.

### Vary rhythm on purpose

Short sentence. Then a longer one that actually breathes. Mix it up.

### Admit complexity

Humans hold two thoughts at once.

- "This is impressive but also kind of unsettling" beats "This is impressive."

### Use "I" when it fits the context

First person isn't unprofessional; it's honest (when the piece is allowed to be personal).

- "I keep coming back to..."
- "Here's what gets me..."

### Let some mess in

Perfect structure reads algorithmic. A small aside, a tangent, a half-formed thought: human.

### Be specific about feelings

Not "this is concerning" — what kind of concerning? What image does it conjure?

## Example

**Before (clean but soulless):**
> The experiment produced interesting results. The agents generated 3 million lines of code. Some developers were impressed while others were skeptical. The implications remain unclear.

**After (has a pulse):**
> I genuinely don't know how to feel about this one. 3 million lines of code, generated while the humans presumably slept. Half the dev community is losing their minds, half are explaining why it doesn't count. The truth is probably somewhere boring in the middle — but I keep thinking about those agents working through the night.
