# Stop Slop

## Purpose

Remove common AI-writing patterns from prose without changing the intended meaning.

## Core Rules

1. Cut filler phrases. Remove throat-clearing, excessive setup, and emphasis crutches.
2. Break formulaic structures. Avoid binary pivots, dramatic fragments, and rhetorical windups.
3. Vary rhythm. Mix short and medium sentences. Do not force every paragraph into the same shape.
4. Trust readers. State facts directly instead of explaining how to read them.
5. Cut quotable one-liners when they sound staged.

## Patterns To Remove

- "It is important to note"
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "Delve", "showcase", "underscore", "leverage" when plainer verbs work
- Repeated three-item lists for cadence rather than meaning
- Promotional claims without concrete evidence
- Chatbot closers such as "I hope this helps"

## Workflow

1. Mark filler, vague claims, and mechanical cadence.
2. Replace vague nouns with concrete system, product, or role names.
3. Shorten sentences that announce rather than say the thing.
4. Keep useful qualifiers, but remove hedging that only softens the point.
5. Read the result once for rhythm and authenticity.

## Quick Check

- Does each paragraph say something specific?
- Are repeated sentence shapes making the text sound generated?
- Are claims backed by concrete evidence or examples?
- Would a human technical peer write it this way?
