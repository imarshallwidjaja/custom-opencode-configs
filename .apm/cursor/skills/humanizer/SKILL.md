# Humanizer

## Purpose

Make prose sound like a real person wrote it while keeping the facts, intent, and audience intact.

Use `stop-slop` first when the main problem is filler, AI-default phrasing, or mechanical structure.

## When To Use

- The draft reads like a press release.
- Claims are generic or overconfident.
- The text overuses polished transitions.
- The tone is deferential, promotional, or too smooth.
- The draft includes chatbot artifacts or meta-commentary.

## Workflow

1. Identify the real audience and situation.
2. Preserve concrete facts, constraints, and commitments.
3. Replace generic claims with specific examples or remove them.
4. Use plain verbs and direct sentence structure.
5. Add natural cadence: short sentences where a human would pause, longer ones where detail matters.
6. Remove over-polished closers and unnecessary reassurance.

## Voice Rules

- Prefer concrete nouns over abstract positioning.
- Keep useful uncertainty: "I think", "seems", and "from what I can tell" are fine when they are honest.
- Do not make casual notes sound like marketing copy.
- Do not make professional writing sound like a chatbot.
- Preserve the user's point of view rather than replacing it with generic authority.

## Output

Provide the rewritten text. If useful, add a short note naming the main patterns removed.
